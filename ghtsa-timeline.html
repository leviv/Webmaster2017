GHTSA TIMELINE

The past ten years of TSA at Waxahachie Global High

Dates

  • 2007 - Global High is founded

  • 2007 - Chapter 501 is established

  • 2009 - Chapter 501's first naitonal competition

  • 2012-2013 - Cardboard boat race champions

  • 2013 - State officer from 501

  • 2015 - New chapter advisor

  • 2016 - Gaylord Opryland National Convention

  • 2017 - Orlando, Florida national competition

Throughout the years of 2006 and 2007, the building was remodled and opened it's doors as the first STEM academy in Ellis county. In addition, Global was one of the first high schools in the state to offer associates degrees in addition to high school diplomas.

Since its conception, TSA has fit in perfectly with the ideals of Global High. So, in the same year that Global was opened, Chapter 501 was brought to pass by April Moon.

In 2009, members from chapter 501 qualified for the national competition, taking home many awards, including a first place trophy for extemperaneous speech.

At the annual leadership conference in Kerrville, Texas, there is a cardboard boat regatta. For two years in a row, GHTSA was the reigning champions.

Madison Warren became the first member of Chapter 501 to serve in a state leadership position. Madison served as the sergeant-at-arms for Texas TSA.

In 2015, Mr. Warren took over leadership of the TSA club at Waxahachie Global High School. He also reached out to the other TSA club in Waxahachie to ensure cooperation.

At the 2016 national TSA convention, Chapter 501 sent a manufacturing protype team, as well as a webmaster team. Although neither team placed, both the students and advisors had fun and learned a lot.

This year, for the national TSA conference in Florida, Chapter 501 is sending a team of three students to compete in tech bowl!
