Notable curriculum

Naturalized Sciences

  • Biology (9) - A course that teaches students the general workings of the living world. It is generally focused on cellular composition, but also covers other areas such as organism classification.

  • Chemistry (10) - A course focused on teaching students the inner workings of chemical reactions. This includes, but is not limited to, electron configuration, periodic table trends, and chemical equations.

  • DC Biology (11/12) - A course that expands on the knowledge taught in high school biology. The lessons are focused on the nature of life.

  • DC Chemistry (12) - A course that expands on the knowledge taught in high school chemistry. It mainly teaches the following types of chemistry: analytical, bio, inorganic, organic, and physical.

Engineering

  • IED (9) - A course provided by PLTW that teaches students the basic skills necessary to take an engineering course or career like how to properly keep an engineering journal.

  • Civil (10) - A course provided by PLTW that teaches students the basic workings housing and infrastructure. This course is vital for any students interested in becoming civil engineers.

  • DC POE (10) - A course provided by PLTW that teaches students the basics of many engineering concepts like: VEX robotics, physics, and energy sources.

  • Robotics (10/11) - A course provided by PLTW that uses VEX robotics to teach students real life application of electrons.

  • Physics (11) - A course provided by PLTW that teaches students basic understanding of the universe like why things move, when they move, and electromagnetic waves.

    • IT/Electronics

    • PIT (9) - A course that teaches students the beginner skills necessary to optimally use a computer, including education over different programs (like photoshop and publisher).

    • Computer Networking (11/12) - A course where students learn the basic communications between computers and other devices.

    • Digital Electronics (11/12) - A course that teaches students the workings of hardware, including things like boolean algebra and logic gates.

    • Computer Maintenance (11/12) - A course that teaches students how to preventive and troubleshoot errors. This is important for daily life and any careers involving computers.

    • CIM (11/12) - A course provided by PLTW that educates students on the process of manufacturing using machinery and electronics. This is an important course that teaches skills like CAD (computer-aided design) and CAM (computer-aided design).

    • Programming (11/12) - A course that teaches students how to use a variety of programming languages that are key to many professions.

    • Animation (11/12) - A course that teaches students how to manipulate and edit images in a variety of ways.

    • Computer Tech (12) - A course that teaches students the workings of a computer. It functions as a platform for students that are planning on going into the IT field or something like it.
