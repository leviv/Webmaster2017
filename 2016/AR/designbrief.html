Augmented Reality

Augmented reality has been used since the 1960s. In 1968 Ivan Sutherland created the first head mounted display system using computer generated graphics and in 1974 Myron Krueger used artificial reality. He had a lab called videoplace which combined projectors with video cameras to project onscreen silhouettes.

The term Augmented Reality was coined in 1990 by Tom Caudell. One of the earliest functioning Augmented Reality systems was created in 1992 by Louis Rosenberg for the United States Air Force which allowed them to virtually control machinery to perform tasks.

Uses

Past

Augmented Reality has been used for so many things. It was used by Julie Martin for a dance performance incorporating virtual objects onto the stage it has even been used by NASA to pilot spacecrafts from down on Earth.

Present

Some of the most commonly used versions of AR is Google Glass and Snapchat. Google Glass uses a mounted computer attached to a frame of glasses to project a image on a visual display. Snapchat uses AR to track facial features.

Future

Programs are currently in development that will use augmented reality as a powerful educational tool in the classroom. Furthermore, the technology can also be applied to other training scenarios such as surgery, engineering, and more.

Technology

Augmented Reality can be used on smartphones and mobile devices and tablets with AR apps. With PCs and computers. It can also be used on head mounted devices such as lenses and glasses with the right technology.

Anything with a camera and a screen can be utilized for AR. The program just needs to be installed.
