About the website

About

The overwhelming majority of this website was hand-coded from scratch by Levi Villarreal. The languages used were HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Jquery, coded on the text editor Brackets, by Adobe . The website is hosted for free through GitHub, which also provided the domain. A few plugins were used throughout the website, such as the script for the drone movement in the history section, but each of them are open source on GitHub and are listed under the plugins section. Most of the images used are from Pixabay, which has a CC0 Public Domain license, which means that the pictures are free for commercial use and no attribution is required. All images on the TSA side of the website were taken and used with the permission of chapter members. Furthermore, most of the videos used throughout the website are either embedded YouTube videos or free media clips which are also attributed in the video section. All motion elements and graphic designs were made by either Rachel Ussery or Levi Villarreal using Adobe Illustrator. All text was written by either Nick Dalquest or Levi Villarreal, with the research properly cited under the research section.

Process

As soon as our team received the design brief of Unmanned aerial vehicles for this year, we were very excited to begin. We decided to start early in the school year, around October in order to be ready whenever regional competition came around. In our first webmaster meeting, we brainstormed what we wanted the final product to look like, and what our goals were with the project. We also assigned roles and responsibilities for the website, with each member having a specified job. Levi Villarreal had the position of main web designer, Christopher Klinck had the job of secondary programmer, Nick Dalquest was the researcher, and Rachel Ussery was the head of animation and graphics.

At the very beginning of the process, other websites were researched in order to gain an idea of what we would like to include in ours. Whenever it was decided what we wanted to include, sketches were drawn in order to lay out where everything would go on the website. After that the programmers got hard to work creating the basic layout of the website, while the graphics and reseach member began creating content. As more content got finished and refined, the website was slowly but surely finished. All that was left was to fix the bugs and correct the spelling errors.

Problems

As a team, we faced many different problems while working on the website. One of the biggest problems was a lack of communication. With so many members working on the website, it was hard to find a time when all of the members could meet. Because of this, meetings and deadlines were often postponed, which led to long delays in the production of the website. Another communication issue we ran into was the coordination of colors, images, shapes, and more elements. With so many different elements being produced at the same time, it was difficult to make all of the elements fit together in a way that was both aesthetically pleasing and functional.

We also faced many more technical problems while the website was under construction. Most of these came from the programming side of things. One of the main issues was that neither of the programmers were very familiar with JavaScript, so they learn while on the job, which was less than ideal and led to even more problems. As with many other types of coding, certain bugs took hours to fix, when it was then realised that a single semicolon was left out. This lead to much frustration and annoyance during many parts of the project. However, the team was able to overcome all of these problems and deliver a beautiful end result.
