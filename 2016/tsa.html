Global High School TSA

GHTSA Summary

TSA is a non-profit organization within schools that allows students to learn, make, and compete in topics they are interested in. TSA chapters take the study of STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics) beyond the classroom and give students the chance to pursue academic challenges among friends with similar goals and interests. Together, chapter members work on competitive events, attend conferences on the state and national levels, and have a good time raising funds to get there. Chapter organization develops leadership, as members may become officers within state, and even national TSA. Our chapter is committed to help our members explore and succeed in their passions.


Waxahachie Global High School was founded in 2008 as one of the first STEM academies in the region. Global High is an early college high school, and has a project-based approach to learning in most its classes. Since its conception, TSA has fit in perfectly with the ideals of Global High. So, in the same year that Global was opened, Chapter 501 was born. Founded and led by April Moon, the chapter was instantly a huge success. The chapter hosted many community outreach programs, and promoted STEM within the school. The students participating in TSA also achieved success, sending multiple students to state and national competitions. Three years later, Scott Warren became the head advisor for TSA, and was succeeded by Michael Warren for the 2015-2016 school year. This year, TSA is the most successful club in the school, with the highest membership in Chapter 501's history. Hopefully the club will continue to advance at the same rate it is now, and we will see even more success in the future.

GHTSA Undertakings

Chapter 501 is undoubtedly one of the most dynamic clubs at Global High School. One of the electives offered at the school is a TSA period, which allows students an hour and a half each period to focus on their TSA projects. This gives students more time to work on other clubs and activities outside of school outside of TSA. Many of the members of Chapter 501 actively participate in robotics, Interact, NHS, science club, and more.


In the past Global High has been an active participant in the Relay for Life, which is a charity sponsored by TSA. The club raised 4000-6000 dollars, with over 30 members participating in the event. The current president plans on re-introducing the tradition next year, and has plans in place to do so.


TSA students have enjoyed a great deal of success throughout the history of the club. Each year for the past eight years, Global has had at least one student advance to national competition. Over 80 percent of the students who compete make it to at least state competition, which means almost all TSA members have a chance of making it to nationals.

TSA members create teams and problem solve to build a mousetrap catapult

One thing implemented by the current officer team is the introduction of problem solving challenges in each chapter meeting. In the challenges, the members are given a problem and a set of materials, and asked to present a solution. In the video above, TSA members were challenged to create a catapult that would launch a ping-pong ball, and the ball that went the furthest would win. They were given a mousetrap, popsicle sticks, paper, tape, and string. These challenges helped the club grow closer together this year, while encouraging a spirit of friendly competition.


Every year since the club began, Chapter 501 has been able to attend the TSA leadership conference at Kerrville. At this conference, officers learn and develop skills that will make them more effective leaders. In addition to lectures, seminars and team-building activities, there is also the annual cardboard boat race. Chapter 501 has often gotten first place in the regatta, although the title has been stripped from us the past two years.
