Tech Education

Waxahachie Global High is one of seven schools in the state of Texas that is both an Early College High School and a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) Academy. Global High prepares high-school students for college level work by integrating 21st century skills in all classes (e.g. - creativity, teamwork, critical thinking). Partnering with Navarro College and UT Tyler, students have the opportunity to graduate from high-school with an Associate's degree. Global High focuses on math, science, technology and engineering, which are fields of study that the average student only briefly explores.

Biology

The biology course at WGHS teaches students the general makeup of the world. In addition to the way we interact with the world. This course focuses on the cellular makeup of the world. Dr. Lester and Mrs. Wood teach this class to freshmen.

Chemistry

This course focuses on explaining the basics of chemistry. Students learn about the chemical reactions, this includes the electron configuration, periodic table trends, and chemical equations. Dr. Lester teaches this class to sophomores.

Physics

This class teaches students about the studies of the forces and movement of things. Focusing on the movements of objects and inertia. This class allows students to better understand the world they live and interact with daily. Mrs. Casey teaches this class to juniors.

DC Biology

This course teaches students more complex lessons about our living world. Students learn more about the interaction of cellular systems and go more in-depth on the cellular makeup of the world. Students learn about how creatures and microorganisms live in their natural habit. Mrs. Restivo teaches this class to seniors.

DC Chemistry

This course goes more into a more in-depth of difficult complexes of chemistry. With an interactive class learning style students in this class have the opportunity to safely get a hands-on experience. This course focuses on labs and studies of the chemical reactions as they happen. Mrs. Restivo teaches this class to seniors..

Introduction to Engineering and Design

Introduction to engineering and design, this class introduces students into the basic concepts of engineering. They learn the basic of an engineering notebook, how to clearly sketch and annotate drawings and how to use Inventor. This class is taught by Mr. Mathenson to freshmen.

Civil Engineering

This class teaches the basics of architectural design and how to safely design and construct a building. Students learn about martials and historic designs. This class is taught by Mrs. Moon to sophomores and juniors.

Principles of Engineering

Principles of engineering, this course teaches students more advanced concepts of the engineering world. This class combines engineering and physics to create an interactive and effective learning environment. This class is taught by Mrs. Casey to sophomores.

Robotics

A course provided by PLTW that uses VEX robotics to teach students real life application of electrons.

Computer Integrated Mechanics

This course is provided to WGHS by PLTW that educates students on the process of manufacturing using machinery and electronics. This is an important course that teaches students skills such as CAD (computer-aided design) and CAM (computer-aided design). This class is taught by Mr. Warren to juniors and seniors.

Digital Electronics

A course that introduces students to the workings of hardware found in computer and other electronic devices. Students have interactive lessons about Boolean algebra and logic gates. This class is taught by Mr. Warren to juniors and seniors.

Algebra

This class, taught by Mrs. Dulworth, is offered mainly to freshman who did no thave the chance to take an algebra class while in middle school. In the class students learn graphs, equations, and calculator applications.

Geometry

Geometry is a math class offered to both freshman and sophomores that focuses on shapes and their properties. While in this course, students undertake projects, such as making a theoretical satellite, to put their knowledge to pracitcal use.

Trigonometry

This course allows students to learn and absorb the lessons that they are taught. They are introduced to complex ideas and equations involving calculations involving triangles and circles. This class is taught by Mrs. Sikes to juniors.

Calculus

This course is where algebra geometry and trigonometry all meet. This class provides students with an extraordinary understanding of the world of mathematics. This class is taught by Mrs. Sikes to seniors.

Statistics

This course introduces students to percentages and probability. This class teaches students how to collect and analyze date in real world applications. This class is taught by Mrs. Sikes to seniors.

Principles of Information Technology

Principles of Internet Technology, a dual-credit course that teaches students the beginner skills necessary to optimally use a computer, internet safety and ethics, copyright, and education over different programs such as Photoshop and Publisher. This class is taught by Mr. Allen to the freshmen.

Networking

In this interactive course, students learn the basic communication that occurs between computers and between other devices. This class is taught by Mr. Enriquez to juniors and seniors.

Computer Maintenance

A course that teaches students how to not only identify but also to preventive and troubleshoot errors. This class is very important for the daily lives of alumni no matter what career they decide to go into as it will most likely involve computers. This class is taught by Mr. Warren to juniors and seniors.

Programming

A course that teaches students how to use a variety of programming languages, such as Scratch and others, which are key to many professions. This class is taught by Mr. Warren to juniors and seniors.

Animation

A course that teaches students how to manipulate and edit images in Blender and other programs. You learn how to create three-dimensional images and create short films. This class is taught by Mr. Warren to juniors and seniors.
