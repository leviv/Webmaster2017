GLOBAL TIMELINE

The history of Global High School spanning 100 years

Dates

  • 1917 - Global is built

  • 1939 - Gym is added

  • 1961 - Science wing added

  • 1969 - WWII memorial built

  • 1970 - Became a middle school

  • 1990 - Became an elementary school

  • 1999 - Became an AEP campus

  • 2007 - Became a STEM academy

  • 2017 - The buildings 100th anniversary

In 1917 the school was built on a beautiful plot of land. The building marked the first public high school available in the city of Waxahachie. This was where the story of this historical campus began.

The gymnasium was added by the WPA in 1939, a wonderful addition to the school that promoted sports and excercise and became a hub of activity for many school dances to come.

In 1961 the science wing was added to the left side of the building. The original wing included two science labs, and two other other rooms that eventually became computer labs.

In 1969, the class of 1944 planted 12 oak trees in the front of the school in memoriam of the men who fought and died in WWII, sacrificing their lives for their country.

The building became a middle school in 1970, named after T.C. Wilemon, a superintendent who served WISD for decades. This is also the year that the school district became desegragated.

During the 1990's, the building was used for the first time as an elementary school.

In 1999, the building became a storage / AEP campus. Because the building was not used as often as it had been, it fell into disrepair and was not kept in a good state.

Throughout the years of 2006 and 2007, the building was remodled and opened it's doors as the first STEM academy in Ellis county. In addition, Global was one of the first high schools in the state to offer associates degrees in addition to high school dimplomas.

After decades of history, and extensive use by the citizens of Waxahachie, the historic T.C. Wilemon building turned 100 years old!
