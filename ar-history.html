AR History

How Augmented Reality progressed and evolved over the decades.

1968

First AR Created

TThe first use of augmented reality was created by Ivan Sutherland, an American scientist known as the Father of Computer Graphics. He created the first head mounted display system by using computer generated graphics to show simple wireframe drawings to users.

1974

AR Laboratory

Myron Krueger built an artificial reality laboratory. The lab was called Videoplace which combined projectors with video cameras to project onscreen silhouettes. It surrounded the user with an interactive environment.

1990

"AR" Coined

The term Augmented Reality was coined by a Boeing researcher Tom Caudell. He created the term to describe the digital display that aircraft electricians used to combine virtual graphics and the physical reality.

1992

First AR System

One of the earliest functioning Augmented Reality systems was created by Louis Rosenberg for the United States Air Force. It was called Virtual Fixtures and allowed them to virtually control machinery to perform tasks. An upper-body exoskeleton allowed the Air Force to control virtually guided machinery so they could perform tasks from a distant and remote operating space.

1994

AR Theatre

The first augmented reality Theatre production was produced by Julie Martin. Called “Dancing in Cyberspace” it combined acrobats who danced with and in virtual objects.
